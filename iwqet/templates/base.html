What is it?

Mit'mit'a is an experimental application to help translate from English to Amharic. In its final form, it will have two components: a rudimentary automatic translator (ተርጓሚ Tergwami) and a translation memory (ማከማቻ Makemacha). In the current version, it consists of the translator only.

Mit'mit'a was designed to be used mainly by people who know both Amharic and English. Users of the system enter texts that they to translate, and Tergwami proposes translations for phrases in the texts. The users can accept or reject the suggestions of the system, entering their own translations if they want. The system keeps a memory of the texts and the translations created by the users, and users have the option of saving the text at any point during the process.

How does it work?

The translator Tergwami has a bilingual dictionary of phrases and basic knowledge of the grammar of English, the grammar of Amharic, and the relationship between them. For each English sentence entered by the user, it first analyzes the words, extracting their roots and grammatical properties. It think searches for phrases in the analyzed sentence that match phrases in its dictionary. Finally, for each phrase that it matches it produces one or more Amharic translations, using its knowledge of Amharic grammar and orthography. In the interface to Mit'mit'a, these results are displayed for the user. When the user corrects the output of the system or replaces its translation with a different one, Tergwami learns from this feedback, improving its dictionary and possibly also its grammatical knowledge. In addition, Mit'mit'a adds the translation given by the user to Makemacha, its translation memory, so that the translation can be used in future sessions when another user is translating a similar sentence.
